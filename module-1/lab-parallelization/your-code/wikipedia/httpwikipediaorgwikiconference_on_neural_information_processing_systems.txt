meeting was first proposed in 1986 as NIPS at the annual invitation-only Snowbird Meeting on <a href="/wiki/Artificial_neural_network" title="Artificial neural network">Neural Networks</a> for Computing organized by <a href="/wiki/The_California_Institute_of_Technology" class="mw-redirect" title="The California Institute of Technology">The California Institute of Technology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bell_Laboratories" class="mw-redirect" title="Bell Laboratories">Bell Laboratories</a>.  NIPS was designed as a complementary open interdisciplinary meeting for researchers exploring biological and artificial <a href="/wiki/Artificial_neural_network" title="Artificial neural network">Neural Networks</a>.  Reflecting this multidisciplinary approach, NIPS began in 1987 with information theorist <a href="/wiki/Ed_Posner" title="Ed Posner">Ed Posner</a> as the conference president and learning theorist <a href="/wiki/Yaser_Abu-Mostafa" title="Yaser Abu-Mostafa">Yaser Abu-Mostafa</a> as program chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Research presented in the early NIPS meetings included a wide range of topics from efforts to solve purely engineering problems to the use of computer models as a tool for understanding biological nervous systems.  Since then, the biological and artificial systems research streams have diverged, and recent NeurIPS proceedings have been dominated by papers on <a href="/wiki/Machine_learning" title="Machine learning">machine learning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a>.\n</p><p>From 1987 until 2000 NIPS was held in <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>, United States. Since then, the conference was held in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, Canada (2001\xe2\x80\x932010), <a href="/wiki/Granada" title="Granada">Granada</a>, Spain (2011), and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Tahoe" title="Lake Tahoe">Lake Tahoe</a>, United States (2012\xe2\x80\x932013). In 2014 and 2015, the conference was held in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, Canada, in Barcelona, Spain in 2016, in Long Beach, United States in 2017, in Montreal, Canada in 2018 and Vancouver, Canada in 2019.  Reflecting its origins at <a href="/wiki/Snowbird,_Utah" title="Snowbird, Utah">Snowbird, Utah</a>, the meeting was accompanied by workshops organized at a nearby ski resort up until 2013, when it outgrew ski resorts. In 2018, the abbreviation was changed to NeurIPS.\n</p><p>The first NeurIPS Conference was sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/IEEE" class="mw-redirect" title="IEEE">IEEE</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The following NeurIPS Conferences have been organized by the NeurIPS Foundation, established by <a href="/wiki/Ed_Posner" title="Ed Posner">Ed Posner</a>. <a href="/wiki/Terrence_Sejnowski" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrence Sejnowski">Terrence Sejnowski</a> has been the president of the NeurIPS Foundation since 1993, when Posner had a bicycle accident. The board of trustees consists of previous general chairs of the NeurIPS Conference.\n</p><p>The first <a href="/wiki/Proceedings" class="mw-redirect" title="Proceedings">proceedings</a> was published in book form by the <a href="/wiki/American_Institute_of_Physics" title="American Institute of Physics">American Institute of Physics</a> in 1987, and was entitled <i>Neural Information Processing Systems</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> then the <a href="/wiki/Proceedings" class="mw-redirect" title="Proceedings">proceedings</a> from the following conferences have been published by <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Kaufmann" class="mw-redirect" title="Morgan Kaufmann">Morgan Kaufmann</a> (1988\xe2\x80\x931993), <a href="/wiki/MIT_Press" title="MIT Press">MIT Press</a> (1994\xe2\x80\x932004) and Curran Associates (2005\xe2\x80\x93present) under the name <i>Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems</i>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_abbreviation_of_the_name">Official abbreviation of the name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conference_on_Neural_Information_Processing_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Official abbreviation of the name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Conference organizers considered abandoning the conference name<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> because of a slang connotation of its abbreviation, NIPS, with the word <i><a href="/wiki/Nipple" title="Nipple">nipples</a></i> and as a <a href="/wiki/Nip" title="Nip">slur against Japanese</a>. After a comment period and survey of 2,270 conference participants, conference organizers decided to keep the name and instead considered changing the acronym, since that was the main concern.<sup id="cite_ref-officialNameChange_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-officialNameChange-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a
